The Launch System: From Idea to Operating Business in 90 Days

By Anas Elimam · 2026

Most launches in Saudi take 9 to 18 months. The market study takes 3 months. The brand takes 2. The website takes another 2. Then licensing. Then hiring. Then the founder is exhausted and revenue still hasn't started.

We ship businesses in 90 days. Not because we work harder. Because the system has a different shape.

What the 90-day system actually does

Month 1 - Foundation: market study (real, sourced), feasibility (real numbers, 3-year projection), licensing pathway. Output: a business that's legally allowed to exist and a financial story that's defensible.

Month 2 - Construction: brand identity, company profile, website, the operational infrastructure (integrations, accounting, CRM scaffolding). Output: a business that looks and operates like a real company on Day 1 of trading.

Month 3. Activation: go-to-market, first customers, first revenue. Output: a business that's earning.

Why most launches fail at 90 days

Three reasons.

One: founders treat launch as a single phase, not three. They try to do month-2 work while month-1 work is incomplete. The foundation cracks.

Two: they outsource the wrong things. Branding gets outsourced (fine), but market study gets done in-house (wrong, it requires structured discipline most founders don't have).

Three: they don't define the launch criterion. "We're live" means different things to different people. We define it as: paying customer, billing system working, support process documented, founder is no longer the only person who can sell.

The MADAR case

MADAR Logistics went from zero to operating revenue in exactly 90 days using this system. Month 1: market study, feasibility, licensing. Month 2: brand, profile, website, six platform integrations live (Salla, Zid, Shopify, Amazon KSA, plus two more). Month 3: first customer cohort onboarded, breakeven trajectory established.

This is the standard. Anything slower than 90 days for a focused launch is a process problem, not an ambition problem.
